About

I am an Associate Professor (Maîtresse de Conférences) in Computational Linguistics at Sorbonne Université (Laboratoire STIH, UFR de Sociologie et Informatique pour les SHS, Paris, France). My work lies at the intersection of artificial intelligence and the analysis of ancient texts. My research focuses primarily on quantitative approaches to textual criticism and on the development of computational linguistic tools for studying the transmission of texts in Late Antiquity.

Research Interests

Quantitative Approaches to Textual Criticism

Applying statistical and computational methods for textual criticism, including manuscript classification, automatic collations and more generally textual analysis. Focus on developing quantitative frameworks for understanding textual transmission, variation patterns, and manuscript relationships, especially for the biblical tradition in its Greek and Latin form.

Computational Linguistics for Ancient and Koinè Greek

Developing specialized NLP tools and linguistic models for Ancient Greek texts. Working on morphological analysis, syntactic parsing, and lemmatization tailored to the specific challenges of late antiquity manuscripts, orthographic variation, and manuscript-specific phenomena like nomina sacra.

Computational Stylometry and Source Criticism

Applying stylometric analysis to authorship attribution and source detection in ancient texts. Research on the Synoptic Problem, Pauline corpus, and other questions of literary composition using statistical methods and machine learning approaches.

Digital Tools for Textual Criticism

Creating digital infrastructure for biblical research, including web applications for manuscript transcription, automatic critical edition generation, and collaborative scholarly workflows. Integration of HTR (Handwritten Text Recognition) with computational analysis pipelines for large-scale manuscript processing and textual analysis.

Teaching

All of my lectures are freely available on my GitHub.
Master 1 (M1)

Formal Grammar

Master Langue et Informatique

Introduction to formal grammar theory, covering context-free grammars, parsing algorithms, and their applications to natural language processing. Students learn to analyze linguistic structures using formal computational frameworks.

Licence 3 (L3)

Large Language Models (LLMs)

Licence Sciences du Langage

Introduction to modern large language models, their architecture, training, and applications. Course covers transformer models, prompt engineering, and practical applications of LLMs in linguistic research and humanities scholarship.

Licence 2 (L2)

Algorithms for Social Sciences and Humanities

Licence Sciences du Langage

Foundational course in algorithmic thinking and computational methods for humanities students. Covers basic programming concepts, data structures, and algorithms with applications to textual analysis.
